The right way to Meditate

1. Create a comfortable place

When you start meditating, it's important to choose a quiet and unimpeded place first.
If it's a room where you can stay quiet in the house or an outdoor space,
a space where you can relax your body and mind is good.
And it's also a good way to put small things in the place that can calm and inspire me.
If you set up your own place, you can feel more comfortable and stay focused when you start meditating.

2. Relaxing your body

Please relax your body to get used to the environment for a few minutes before starting meditation.
At this time, simple yoga or stretching together to relieve tension on the face, neck, and shoulders is more effective.
Also, many people think that you should take a cross-legged posture when meditating,
but you don't have to. You can sit on a sofa, chair, or lie on the bed.
Take various poses that you can comfortably do.

3. Securing a fixed time

When practicing meditation, it is recommended to start with a short time of 5 to 10 minutes and gradually increase the time.
Because if you try to meditate for a long time from the beginning, it can be difficult to enjoy the effect because of the psychological burden.
Therefore, when you first start meditating, it is recommended to set the time you want to meditate as a timer and then increase the time
step by step when you get used to it.
Through this, you can reduce the burden of mindfulness meditation and make it a habit steadily.

4. Looking at breathing

When you start meditating, try to focus on your breathing, focusing on your breathing first.
Feeling how breath flows and flows in the body, and taking a deeper and longer breath calms the body and mind.
If you are unconscious from the sounds or misconceptions around you,
come back to your concentration while observing your breathing.
Even if you have negative thoughts and feelings, practice continuously to return to your current breathing.
